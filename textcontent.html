About Me

I'm an Electronics Engineer (EIT) at Kardium working on the Electrical Team. I received my Bachelor of Applied Science specializing in Electronics Engineering from SFU in August 2022.

During my degree and for quite some time after I was involved in the SFU Robot Soccer Club. The club's aim is to create a team of robots able to play a game of soccer using a golf ball on a small field against another team. During my degree I served as the electronics team lead and designed the motherboard for the robots as well as guided the design of the other PCBs interfacing to it. After finishing my degree I transitioned to a mentor and continue to help with troubleshooting and firmware development for the PCBs.

Currently, I am working on a couple of projects: a high voltage (0-500 V, 0-1 A, 500 W) programmable power supply, and a 100 MHz optically isolated oscilloscope probe. The power supply project will have a section on the portfolio sometime later this year. A lot of my project ideas stem from challenges that arise at work. I'm usually too busy to spend enough time learning about these topics in the depth that I want so I find it enjoyable to be able to scratch that itch at home.

In my spare time, I enjoy riding my bike and going to the gym. I like to work out and stay physically active.
